Promoting ADUs

Our project with the Evanston Development Co-op (EDC) involved creating a website for their On to 2022 campaign. EDC is a co-operative based on out of Evanston, IL that campaigns for the legalization of Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) in Chicago and across the country. ADUs, also commonly referred to as granny flats or coach houses, are basically separate residences on the same property as a larger single-family house. Historically, they have been outlawed by zoning ordinances and local governments, but the recently ADUs have gained popularity due to their many social, economic, and environmental benefits. EDC needed a website that would be able to:

1 - increase awareness of the legality of ADUs
2 - promote education about the benefits of ADUs
3 - mobilize campaigners for the legalization of ADUs.

Environmental, Economical, Equitable

ADUs are smaller dwellings located on the same property as a single-family home. Many municipalities across the country have restricted or banned these types of housing. However, ADUs actually bring many benefits to communities, such as additional income for homeowners, increased affordable, equitable housing, reduced urban or suburban sprawl, and minimizing the environmental impact of housing.

Mapping User Journeys

After our initial client interviews, we created user personas and journeys for potential users of the EDC website. We wanted to make sure we were providing relevant information and organizing it in a way that was accessible to the wide audience that EDC was promoting towards. When mapping these user journeys, we focused on identifying who would be accessing this website, what their goals would be, and what message EDC would try to convey to each of them.

Brainstorming Website Structure

After we had a clear idea of EDC's goals and had defined our target audience for this website, we moved into the brainstorming phase. We spent time brainstorming and discussing website architecture and information organization as well as styling, layout, and color. Our initial brainstorms started on paper and slowly increased in fidelity as we converged onto a final design to implement.

Data Visualization of ADU Legality

On this project, I lead the design and development of a data visualization feature that helps users quickly see how ADU-friendly certain states are. While EDC is based in Evanston, IL, our client wanted to not only target residents of Chicagoland, but wanted to provide relevant information to people across the country.

After some initial brainstorming and research, we came up with the idea for an interactive map that displayed different tiers of ADU-friendliness for each state and would not only provide quick access to national ADU guidelines, but would make this page more interactive and fun for the visitors.

Promoting ADU Literacy

A big part of EDC's On to 2022 Campaign was to dispelled common myths about ADUs and increase awareness of their benefits. To do this, we provided easy-to-digest information about what ADUs are and how they can improve the communities they are in on our What are ADUs? page and on our FAQs page. Some common misconceptions include:

1 - ADUs make parking harder to find by increasing population density.

2 - ADUs will decrease property value in the communities they are in.

3 - ADUs are mainly used as vacation or short-term rentals.

These misconceptions are largely non-issues with ADUs. By increasing opportunities for affordable housing, it actually decreases the need for long-distance commuting by allowing people to live nearer to work and commute via public transportation or by foot. Additionally, while ADUs do provide more affordable housing opportunities, they do not decrease property value. In fact, an ADU can actually be seen as an investment for many as the additional living space can increase property value and provide additional income as well. ADUs are also mostly used a permanent or long-term residences, often by members of the community who are looking to downsize or house an aging relative. We included statistics to convey this information is a variety of visual and written ways to our target audience.

According to a 2017 report out of the Terner Center at UC Berkley, 60% of ADUs in Portland and Vancouver were used as permanent residences, while just 12% were used as short-term rentals.

Mobilizing ADU Supporters

An element we included to mobilize supporters of ADUs to take action was a contact form that put the user in touch with EDC. This allows users to easily find out more information about ADU advocacy both in the Chicagoland area as well as across the country. Many times potential ADU advocates may not know how to get involved, as ADUs are normally regulated on a local scale, and legality may change from municipality to municipality. This form streamlines this process for ADU advocates by connecting them to an existing organizing that has lots of resources on how to campaign for ADUS, knows what the current legal status of ADUs in t he areas surrounding Chicago, and has established contacts with local policymakers involved in the ADU legalization effort.
