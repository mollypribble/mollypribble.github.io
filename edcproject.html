Promoting ADUs

Our project with the Evanston Development Co-operative (EDC) involved creating a website for their On to 2022 campaign.

EDC is a co-operative based on out of Evanston, IL that campaigns for the legalization of Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) in Chicago and across the country. EDC needed a website that would be able to:

1 - increase awareness of the legality of ADUs
2 - promote education about the benefits of ADUs
3 - mobilize campaigners for the legalization of ADUs

Environmental, Economical, Equitable

ADUs are smaller dwellings located on the same property as a single-family home.

Many municipalities across the country have restricted or banned these types of housing. However, ADUs actually bring many benefits to communities, such as additional income for homeowners, increased affordable, equitable housing, reduced urban or suburban sprawl, and minimizing the environmental impact of housing.

Mapping User Journeys & Prototyping

User Journeys

After our initial client interviews, we created user personas and journeys for potential users of the EDC website. We wanted to make sure we were providing relevant information and organizing it in a way that was accessible to the wide audience that EDC was promoting towards. We identified who would be accessing this website, what their goals would be, and what message EDC would try to convey to each of them.

Data Visualization of ADU Legality

On this project, I lead the design and development of a data visualization feature that represents how ADU-friendly certain states are.

Our client wanted to not only target residents of Chicagoland, but wanted to provide relevant information to people across the country.

To address this we developed an interactive map that displayed different tiers of ADU-friendliness for each state and would not only provide quick access to national ADU guidelines, but would make this page more interactive and fun for the visitors.

Mobilizing ADU Supporters

An element we included to mobilize supporters of ADUs to take action was a contact form that put the user in touch with EDC.

Many times potential ADU advocates may not know how to get involved, as ADUs are normally regulated on a local scale, and legality may change from municipality to municipality. This form streamlines this process for ADU advocates by connecting them to an existing organizing that has lots of resources on how to campaign for ADUS, knows what the current legal status of ADUs in the areas surrounding Chicago, and has established contacts with local policymakers involved in the ADU legalization effort.
