Raising Awareness of Sudden Cardiac Arrest

For their conference, SaveMiheart needed a logo that would both match their branding as well as illustrate what the process of surviving sudden cardiac arrest looks like.

SaveMiHeart is a nonprofit organization based in Michigan that aims to improve survival from sudden cardiac arrest in the community through research and education initiatives. This year they are virtually hosting their 6th annual conference covering bystander intervention and cardiac arrest survival after the COVID-19 pandemic.

Initial Inspiration

Chain of Survival

My client indicated they would like to include the chain of surviving sudden cardiac arrest in the logo.

Colors

The colors for this project were pulled from SaveMiHeart's existing logo and branding.

EKG Line

The EKG line showing healthy heartbeats also inspired the design of the conference logo.

SaveMiHeart Logo

The SaveMiHeart logo and branding inspiring the look of the conference logo.

Form & Function

This logo would mainly be use as a header for webpages, e-flyers, and emails as well as a Zoom background during the event.

This pointed towards a larger, horizontal layout that could be used as a banner at the top of conference advertisement and communications as well as a Zoom background.

The Importance of Bystander CPR

For every minute that passes without bystander CPR and AED intervention, a victim's chance of survival decreases by 10%.

Some of the initial sketches did not specifically include bystander CPR, a key step to improving cardiac arrest survival.

After getting feedback from my client and learning more about chances of survival with and without this step, I decided to add a pair of CPR hands to the logo and iterated on more designs from there.

Initial Sketches

The initial sketches depicted the chain of survival as part of an EKG line.

Adding Details

CPR hands were added and line width was altered.

Storytelling through Line Width

Original iterations of the logo also seemed too static. Using the SaveMiHeart logo as inspiration, we decided to use a dynamic line width to convery more dimension, movement, and fluidity.

The conference logo is also reminiscent of a pen stroke, like someone was writing their story about how they survived sudden cardiac arrest. One of the campaigns run by SaveMiHeart actually looks for and reports on survival stories from cardiac arrest victims. I felt that this line width almost represents those survivors telling their stories through SaveMiHeart.

The Final Product

Final Logo

The final logo depicts the chain of cardiac arrest survival on an EKG line. The colors are drawn from the colors from the existing SaveMiHeart logo. The final logo also includes the SaveMiHeart logo and slogan text as well as the name of the conference, which includes stylistic details that match the logo.
